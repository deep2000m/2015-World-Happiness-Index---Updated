**🌍 World Happiness Index 2015 — Analysis**

**📘 Overview**
This project analyzes the 2015 World Happiness Index to understand what drives happiness across countries.
It explores how GDP, health, social support, freedom, and governance trust interact to shape well-being.

**🔍 Key Insights**
- Happiness is multi-dimensional → No single factor explains it
- Top countries show balance across GDP, health, freedom, and social support
- Bottom countries face systemic gaps across multiple dimensions
- GDP strongly correlates with happiness, but is not sufficient alone
- Social support is the most stable driver, even in low-GDP regions
- Health acts as a multiplier of overall well-being
- Freedom contributes independently of economic strength
- Governance paradox → Happiness often exists despite low trust
- GDP + Trust together produce the strongest outcomes
- Low GDP + High Trust indicates resilience

**🧠 Key Concept**
- Happiness is not maximized by one factor—it emerges from a balanced system of economic, social, and institutional elements.
📈 Custom Metric
- Happiness Efficiency (6.35)
→ Measures how effectively countries convert socio-economic inputs into happiness

**💡 Recommendations**
- Focus on holistic development, not GDP alone
- Invest in health and social infrastructure
- Improve institutional trust for long-term sustainability
- Incorporate qualitative well-being metrics

**✨ Final Thought**
Wealth and health build happiness—but trust determines how sustainable it is.
